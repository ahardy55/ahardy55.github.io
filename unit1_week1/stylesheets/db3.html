My 3 Favorites Websites

#1. Baseball-reference.com
Why is this one of your favorite sites?
Baseball-reference was born out of the dial-up era. It catered to an audience who wanted to look up baseball statistics without having to wait for long load times caused by unncessary photos, videos and ads. The site grew into the definitive resource for historial baseball statistics. If you want to know what Hank Aaron did on April 18th, 1957, Baseball-reference can tell you (he went 1 for 4 with a home run in a 1-0 victory against the Reds!). It's a seemingly endless wealth of easy-to-access baseball information.

What area of the site is your eye drawn to when looking at the homepage? Stand back further, what area is your eye drawn to now? Is that area the most important area of the site?
Using Baseball-reference religiously for years, I'm mostly drawn to the "search" boxes. The front page actually looks pretty intimidating and cluttered if you aren't used to the site. Taking a step back, I'm drawn more to the Baseball-Reference.com news section, likely because it is one of the sections that is not white or gray. Interestingly, this is the part of the site I look at least often.

How would you describe the website visually? List 5 adjectives. (i.e. "pretty", "elegant", "simple", "dark", "cluttered", "basic" etc)
Cluttered, sterile, intimidating, drab, overwheling. Looks are not what Baseball-reference is about.

What problem does this website solve? What content does it have?
Baseball-reference contains statistics from virtually very player, team, game, season in Major League History (and even a good portion of minor league history). In addition, it allows one to run custom reports with their "season finder" and "player finder" tools and contains a wiki pages of baseball information. It is an extremely easy to use, comprehensive baseball resource. It can be easily navigated in ways other online baseball resources simply cannot and contains far more information than any other baseball site I have seen.

What 5 adjectives would you use to describe the content, focus, and purpose of the site? (i.e. "practical", "fun", "whimsical", "silly", "serious" etc) How does that compare to the adjectives you used to describe the site visually?
Practical, comprehensive, frivilous, historical, purposeful. Again, Baseball-reference isn't about looks, it's about the content.

How easy is it to find what you are looking for from the homepage? How about from another page? The search functions are extremely easy to use, and in-page links make it extremely easy to waste hours on this site. For example, I look up Hank Aaron's career stats. I can then click on a year and see the entire teams stats for that year. I can click on a link to see the league's standings for that year. I can then click on that postseason links for a given year, etc...

How easy is it to browse through all the content of the site?
It would likely be impossible. It contains individual pages for virtually ever player and team in baseball history, including individual game logs.

How do you feel after being on the site for a while? (i.e. "bored", "happy", "anxious or hyper", "like I wasted a lot of time" etc)
Like I wasted an a lot of time. It is extremely easy to get sucked into a baseball-reference wormhole and not emerge for hours.

Does the site sell anything? If so, have you purchased any of it? Why or why not?
They offer a reporting tool for something like $5 a month. If you want to find out which players had the highest batting averages ranging in the seasons from 1956-1965, this could provide that list. I've used it in the past, but mostly I don't. Another website offers a similiar reporting tool for free, so I just use that.
